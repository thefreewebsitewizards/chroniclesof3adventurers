I thought I'd share with you our easy peasy salt dough recipe!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups plain flour
  • 1 cup salt
  • 1 cup of water

Instructions

Step 1: Mix the Dough

Combine all the ingredients together until you get a dough. If it's too sticky add a bit more flour, if it's too dry add a little bit more water.

Step 2: Shape Your Ornaments

On a floured worktop roll out your dough, use cookie cutters to make your shapes or you can press hands to make prints.

ðŸ’¡ Important:

Make sure you add a hole before baking - this is for when you need to string up your decoration.

Step 3: Add Personal Touches

We use fondant icing letter stamps to make names and dates on our salt dough ornaments.

Step 4: Baking

Once you've made your decorations, place them onto a lined baking tray into a preheated oven at 150Â°C and bake for 2-2:40 hours.

ðŸ”¥ Baking Tips:

  • They should turn a golden colour when thoroughly dried out
  • If you tap the bottom they should sound hollow

Step 5: Cooling & Painting

Leave to cool - we left ours overnight. Once cooled you can paint. We found acrylic works best.

You can also add details using a marker pen or glitter.

Step 6: Sealing

Once finished we recommend to seal using PVA glue or Mod Podge. You need to seal both sides leaving to dry overnight each side.

Step 7: Final Touch

Final step is to attach string, ribbon or twine to ornament ready for hanging.
