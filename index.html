Play Categories

Explore our collection of play ideas designed to spark creativity and joy

Outdoor Play

Nature-based activities that encourage exploration and adventure in the great outdoors.

Outdoor Play Ideas

Nature Scavenger Hunts: Create lists of items for children to find in your garden or local park - leaves, rocks, flowers, or specific colors.

Mud Kitchen Adventures: Set up an outdoor kitchen with old pots, pans, and utensils for imaginative cooking with natural materials.

Garden Exploration: Plant seeds, water plants, and observe how things grow. Perfect for teaching responsibility and patience.

Obstacle Courses: Use garden furniture, ropes, and natural elements to create fun physical challenges.

Weather Watching: Observe clouds, collect rainwater, or make wind catchers to learn about weather patterns.

Sensory Play

Engaging activities that stimulate the senses and support developmental growth.

Sensory Play Ideas

Texture Exploration: Create sensory bins with rice, pasta, beans, or sand for tactile discovery and fine motor development.

Playdough Creations: Homemade playdough with different scents, colors, and textures to engage multiple senses.

Water Play: Simple water tables, pouring activities, and floating experiments that soothe and engage.

Sound Bottles: Fill containers with different materials to create various sounds and rhythms.

Sensory Bags: Sealed bags filled with gel, glitter, or small objects for safe tactile exploration.

Messy Play

Creative and liberating activities that embrace the beautiful chaos of childhood.

Messy Play Ideas

Paint Exploration: Finger painting, brush painting, and painting with unconventional tools like sponges or vegetables.

Slime Making: Safe, homemade slime recipes that provide satisfying tactile experiences.

Foam Fun: Shaving cream or soap foam for sensory play and mark-making activities.

Jelly Play: Edible jelly for safe messy play, especially great for younger children.

Cornflour Goop: The classic oobleck recipe that's both liquid and solid - endlessly fascinating!

Saturday Bakes

Family-friendly recipes that bring everyone together in the kitchen for delicious fun.

Saturday Bakes Ideas

Simple Cookies: Easy sugar cookies or gingerbread that children can help mix, roll, and decorate.

No-Bake Treats: Chocolate rice crispy cakes, energy balls, and fridge cakes that require no oven time.

Bread Making: Simple bread recipes where little hands can help knead and shape the dough.

Muffin Madness: Basic muffin recipes with endless flavor variations - perfect for using up fruit.

Pizza Dough Fun: Making pizza from scratch, letting kids choose their own toppings and create edible art.

Our Story

Hi! I'm a mum of two amazing boys, and this blog is our family's journey through the beautiful chaos of childhood. When my eldest was diagnosed with CLN1, our world shifted, but it also opened our eyes to the incredible resilience and joy that children possess.

Here, you'll find our adventures in play, honest book reviews, weekend baking experiments, and the real, unfiltered moments of family life. I believe in the power of simple pleasures – a good book, a messy craft project, or the perfect Saturday morning pancakes.

As a self-published author, I'm passionate about storytelling and creating content that celebrates the magic in everyday moments. Join us as we explore, create, and find joy in the little things.
