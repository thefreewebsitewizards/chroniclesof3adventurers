So we are knee deep into the summer holidays. We are approaching week three and I thought I would do a little blog about summer, the pressures of summer and some of the things that we're doing to take the pressure off.

üíö First off, be kind to yourself you are doing the best you can.

Summer isn't all perfect Instagram squares, or that everyone goes on holiday and spends a fortune entertaining kids.

For us this year, we're not even going away for a staycation we're just doing a few days out with the kids. I've planned that two days a week we go for days out, can be paid days out free days out for a picnic at the park. Anything just two days a week. The rest of the week are things like playing at home in the garden or baking and this really helped to take the pressure off having a perfect 24/7 non-stop summer.

This might be surprising, but this is one of the more full on weeks where we did a little bit more than usual, but it doesn't seem like a lot does it?

I believe that children still need a bit of structure and stability to their day to day during summer or half terms with a few nice days out included. As much as you crave to be at home having a relaxing day, children need that too, they need to use their imagination they need to have their creative outlet. They need to chill.

üåü Important Reminder

This is my reminder to parents to take the pressure off of yourselves during summer, children do not need to be entertained 24 hours a day seven days a week. The most important part is making memories and usually those memories come out of sporadic moments.

What do you remember from your childhood vacations? I remember baking with my Nanna, picking potatoes with my Grandad and having picnics with my mum. Small little things that will stay with us because it's a time that we have together.
